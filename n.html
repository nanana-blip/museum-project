Gallery

Explore our collection of masterpieces

The Starry Night

Vincent van Gogh, 1889. Oil on canvas depicting a swirling night sky over a village.

Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci, 1503–1519. Perhaps the most famous portrait in the world, housed in the Louvre.

The Kiss

Gustav Klimt, 1907–1908. A golden masterpiece of Art Nouveau, full of intricate patterns and tenderness.

Girl with a Pearl Earring

Johannes Vermeer, c. 1665. Known as the "Mona Lisa of the North," a striking tronie portrait.

Christina's World

Andrew Wyeth, 1948. A hauntingly beautiful tempera painting of a woman gazing across a vast field.

The Garden of Earthly Delights

Hieronymus Bosch, c. 1490–1510. A triptych teeming with fantastical imagery, sin, and paradise.

Artists

The brilliant minds behind the masterpieces

Vincent van Gogh

Dutch Post-Impressionist painter whose vivid colors and emotional honesty made him one of history's most influential artists.

Leonardo da Vinci

Italian Renaissance polymath and painter, celebrated for his unparalleled mastery of light, anatomy, and human expression.

Gustav Klimt

Austrian symbolist painter and a leading figure of the Vienna Secession movement, known for his decorative golden style.

Johannes Vermeer

Dutch Golden Age painter renowned for his intimate domestic interiors and extraordinary command of light and color.
